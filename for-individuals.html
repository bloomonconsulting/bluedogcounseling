Pet loss and grief support

The loss of an animal family member is a significant loss. The grief that comes with it, including the grief that starts before death during illness, treatment decisions, or end-of-life planning, is real and clinically meaningful. BLUE dog provides individual consultation grounded in clinical expertise in grief and the human-animal bond.

What brings people here

The experiences individual consultation addresses

Pet loss and grief

Grief after the death of an animal family member is real loss. Individual consultation provides structured, clinically informed support for processing that grief, including grief that others in your life may minimize or not understand.

Euthanasia decisions

Deciding when and whether to euthanize a beloved animal is one of the most difficult decisions a person can face. The weight of that decision, and the grief and guilt that often follow, deserve clinical attention. Consultation provides space to process both the decision and what comes after it.

Terminal diagnosis and caregiving

When an animal family member receives a serious diagnosis, the anticipatory grief and caregiving stress that follows is significant. Individual consultation can help you understand your options, manage that stress, and identify a support plan while your animal is still with you.

Complex or unacknowledged grief

Grief after animal loss is frequently minimized by the people around you. That minimization compounds the loss. Consultation provides a space where the significance of the relationship and the grief are taken seriously from the start.

The service

Individual consultation

A 60-minute virtual session with a clinician who specializes in grief and the human-animal bond. This is a short-term, structured service, not ongoing therapy. It is designed to provide clarity, a concrete support plan, and specific next steps for whatever you are navigating.

The session includes time to review your situation in clinical terms, ask questions, and identify the most useful resources and supports. Sessions are virtual and available to individuals across the country.

The grief associated with pet loss and end-of-life decisions is frequently minimized in standard clinical settings. Individual consultation provides a structured space where the clinical weight of that loss is taken seriously.
