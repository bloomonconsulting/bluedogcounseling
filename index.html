For animal care professionals

Built specifically for the people doing this work

Veterinarians, vet techs, shelter staff, and others working in animal care face a specific category of occupational stress that requires a specialized response. BLUE dog's consulting and training is built on clinical expertise in grief, animal loss, and the human-animal bond, applied directly to the animal care workplace.

See what we offer providers
  • Moral stress and secondary trauma We address the specific clinical realities of working with animal death, euthanasia, and client grief daily.
  • Staff retention and team sustainability Practical consulting that addresses the operational factors driving turnover in veterinary and shelter settings.
  • Grief-informed client communication Training that gives your team a structured approach to supporting clients through animal death, euthanasia, and diagnosis conversations.
  • Evidence-based approach All consulting and training are grounded in clinical best practices specific to grief, animal loss, and the animal care workplace.

The practice

Founded on a recognized gap in the field

Cōlleen O'Brien, LMSW, has been doing pet loss and grief counseling at BLUE dog since 2009. Through that clinical work, she saw the same gap repeatedly: animal care providers with nowhere to turn for support that named what they were actually experiencing. BLUE dog's consulting and training grew directly from that recognition. Since 2010, Cōlleen has facilitated the pet loss support group at the Humane Society of Huron Valley, and that partnership continues to ground the work.
