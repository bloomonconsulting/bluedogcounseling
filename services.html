For organizations

Organizational consulting

We work directly with leadership in veterinary practices, animal shelters, and animal welfare organizations to identify the specific operational and cultural factors driving moral stress and secondary trauma in their teams.

Consulting engagements are customized. Every engagement begins with an assessment of your organization's specific environment, staff composition, and the types of animal death and euthanasia your team encounters regularly.

Consulting areas include:

  • Staff wellness assessment and gap analysis
  • Grief-informed policy development
  • Euthanasia protocol review and team support structures
  • Secondary trauma identification and organizational response
  • Leadership training on managing moral stress in direct reports
  • Retention strategy development tied to staff sustainability

For individuals

Individual consultation

A 60-minute virtual session for individuals navigating pet loss, difficult medical decisions, behavioral decisions, or caregiving stress. This is a short-term service: a structured, clinically informed session focused on clarity, resource planning, and a concrete support plan.

The consultation provides a structured opportunity to ask clinical questions, review the situation, and identify specific next steps. Sessions are virtual and available to individuals across the country.

The grief and stress associated with pet loss and end-of-life decisions are frequently minimized in standard clinical settings. Individual consultation provides a structured, clinically informed approach to navigating that process.

For mental health professionals

Supervision & case consultation

Clinical training on the human-animal bond, animal loss, and veterinary trauma is rare. When clients bring these experiences into session, specialized knowledge is required to respond effectively.

Blue Dog Counseling provides supervision and case consultation for therapists, counselors, and social workers encountering animal loss, euthanasia trauma, and animal welfare grief in their practice.

Supervision covers:

  • Clinical approaches to animal loss and human-animal bond grief
  • Euthanasia trauma in clients who are animal care providers
  • Secondary trauma in veterinary and shelter worker clients
  • Specialized grief models applicable to animal loss
