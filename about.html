About BLUE dog

Grief-informed consulting and training for veterinary practices, animal shelters, and everyone who works with animals.

The gap we address

Specialized support for the animal care workforce

The bond between humans and animals is one of the most significant connections in life. The grief, stress, and complex decisions associated with that bond, particularly in professional animal care settings, are consistently underaddressed by standard mental health training and generic workplace wellness programs.

BLUE dog was founded specifically to bridge that gap. We provide grief-informed consulting, training, and clinical services built around the actual occupational experiences of veterinarians, vet techs, shelter workers, animal control officers, and anyone else who works with animals.

Our expertise is developed specifically for the human-animal bond, animal loss, and the animal care workplace.

Founder

Cōlleen O'Brien, LMSW

Cōlleen has been doing pet loss and grief counseling at BLUE dog since 2009. Through that work, animal care providers came to her as clients, and what she saw was consistent: a workforce carrying specific, serious occupational stress that was consistently underaddressed. Euthanasia decision-making, moral stress, and secondary traumatic stress as things that happen to people doing this work professionally and repeatedly were rarely the focus of mainstream mental health training or standard workplace wellness programs. That gap became the foundation for Blue Dog's consulting and training.

Since 2010, Cōlleen has facilitated the pet loss support group at the Humane Society of Huron Valley, the first pet loss support group in Washtenaw County. That partnership has sustained a direct connection to what people navigating animal loss are actually experiencing, and to the animal care staff doing that work alongside them.

Cōlleen's understanding of this work is personal as well as clinical. She has shared her life with animals, navigated complex illness and medical decisions, adopted from shelters, and made euthanasia decisions for dogs she loved. The work she does is informed by that lived understanding alongside the clinical training.

How we work

Our approach

We start from a position of field knowledge

Animal care professionals arrive to an engagement where the significance of their work and their relationship to the animals in their care is already understood. Our consulting and clinical services are built on that foundation.

We are evidence-based

All consulting, training, and supervision is grounded in clinical best practices in grief, trauma, and human-animal bond research, developed specifically for animal care settings.

We work at every level of the organization

From individual animal care providers to veterinary staff and shelter leadership, effective support requires addressing both the people doing the work and the organizational conditions they work within.
