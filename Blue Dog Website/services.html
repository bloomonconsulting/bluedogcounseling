For organizations

Organizational consulting

We work directly with leadership in veterinary practices, animal shelters, and animal welfare organizations to identify the specific operational and cultural factors driving moral stress and secondary trauma in their teams.

Consulting engagements are customized. We do not use a generic wellness framework. Every engagement begins with an assessment of your organization's specific environment, staff composition, and the types of animal death and euthanasia your team encounters regularly.

Consulting areas include:

  • Staff wellness assessment and gap analysis
  • Grief-informed policy development
  • Euthanasia protocol review and team support structures
  • Secondary trauma identification and organizational response
  • Leadership training on managing moral stress in direct reports
  • Retention strategy development tied to staff sustainability

For teams

Staff training

Role-specific training for veterinarians, vet techs, shelter workers, and animal control officers. Our training is not general workplace wellness content; it addresses the specific occupational experiences of people whose work involves animal death, euthanasia, and daily exposure to client grief.

Training is available in-person or virtually, and can be delivered as a standalone session or as part of a broader consulting engagement.

Training topics include:

  • Recognizing secondary traumatic stress and moral injury in animal care roles
  • Communicating with clients around animal death, terminal diagnosis, and euthanasia
  • Supporting colleagues following difficult cases or decisions
  • Creating sustainable boundaries in high-exposure roles
  • Understanding the grief process specific to animal loss

Single and multi-session

Workshops

Workshops are available for individual organizations, veterinary conferences, and animal welfare associations. Sessions are designed to deliver practical, applicable content in a structured format; not motivational content or general self-care guidance.

Workshops can be delivered as standalone events or as a series. All workshops are adapted to the specific discipline and setting of participants.

Grief-informed client communication

A structured approach to conversations around animal death, terminal diagnosis, and euthanasia. Participants leave with specific communication tools applicable to their role.

Audience: Veterinarians, vet techs, client service staff

Secondary trauma in animal care

An evidence-based session on recognizing, naming, and responding to secondary traumatic stress in veterinary and shelter environments. Addresses both individual and organizational responses.

Audience: All animal care roles

Euthanasia decision support

A workshop for veterinary professionals on the clinical and psychological dimensions of euthanasia decisions, for clients and for the practitioners involved. Addresses moral stress and decision fatigue directly.

Audience: Veterinarians, vet techs, shelter leadership

Building sustainable teams

Designed for practice owners, clinic managers, and shelter directors. Covers the relationship between staff moral stress, burnout, and retention, and outlines concrete organizational responses.

Audience: Leadership and management

For individuals

Individual consultation

A 60-minute virtual session for individuals navigating animal loss, difficult medical decisions, behavioral decisions, or caregiving stress. This session provides clarity, resource planning, and a concrete support plan. It is not ongoing therapy.

The consultation provides a validated space to ask questions, process the experience, and identify specific next steps. Sessions are virtual and available to individuals across the country.

The grief and stress associated with animal loss and end-of-life decisions are frequently minimized in standard clinical settings. Individual consultation provides a structured, clinically informed approach to navigating that process.

For mental health professionals

Supervision & case consultation

Most clinical training programs do not address the human-animal bond, pet loss, or veterinary trauma. When clients bring these experiences into session, many therapists lack the specific knowledge base to respond effectively.

Blue Dog Counseling provides supervision and case consultation for therapists, counselors, and social workers encountering pet loss, euthanasia trauma, and animal welfare grief in their practice.

Supervision covers:

  • Clinical approaches to pet loss and human-animal bond grief
  • Euthanasia trauma in clients who are animal care providers
  • Secondary trauma in veterinary and shelter worker clients
  • Specialized grief frameworks applicable to animal loss
